Research experience

Mars 2021 - October 2022 | Postdoctoral researcher | National Research Institute for Agriculture, Food and EnvironmentINRAE, Sophia-Antipolis, FR

Details

During my stay at the Institut Sophia AgrobiotechISA, I was involved in the BIDIME project. Briefly, this project aimed to develop innovation in biological pest control solutions and transfer it to farmers and land managers.
More particularly, I joined Vincent CALCAGNOs lab to investigate the variability of movements of insect groups to temperature among 35 populations sampled along a latitude gradient.
Here I mainly collaborated with Michela ION SCOTTA who reared the populations of small parasitoid fly named Trichogramma. Michela has also video-recorded the insect groups during steady decreases/increases in temperature (2 hours, see fig.1).

Figure 1: Temperature ramps over which flies movements were recorded and analyzed.

Once the videos were recorded, I used video-tracking solutions to retrieve flies movements over the temperature ramps.
I then analyzed the movements of the 35 flies populations to test for the variability in thermal biology and movements. My aim here was to uncover potential patterns of local adaptation (i.e., relationship between resistance and the bioclimatic area of the origin of the populations) and test for the existence of adaptive trade-offs between resistance to cold and warm temperatures.

Besides this work also inspired the development of an package (MoveR) to easily filter, clean and analyze movement data (Stay tuned for upcoming release).

February 2015 - May 2015 | M.Sc. candidate | Bourgogne-Franche-Comt UniversityUBFC, Besanon, FR

Details

During my first year of master I conducted an internship with Frdric GIMBERT and Lotfi ALEYA to work on the bioavailability of trace metal elements in a landfill leachate collection pond. Indeed, until 2002 household waste disposal was authorized in France, resulting in contamination of the surrounding environment, primarily through contaminants leaching. To reduce environmental contamination entailed by this mode of waste storage, the Etueffont landfill (study site) set up a lagoon ponds system draining landfill leachates.
Here, we aimed to better understand how the leaching of trace metal elements may affect contaminant accumulation in biota (i.e., bioaccumulation kinetics) and how water and sediment compartments may contribute to the observed bioaccumulation patterns.
To tackle these aims, we used an active biomonitoring approach through caging laboratory-raised midges larvae (Chironomus riparius) in the first landfill leachate collection pond. More particularly, we used two types of cages to either expose the larvae to water only or water and sediment for a five days maximum duration.
Back in the laboratory, we performed subcellular fractionation of the midges to measure trace metal bioaccumulation in the cytosolic (i.e., sensitive fraction) and insoluble fractions. We then modeled the bioaccumulation kinetic for each contaminant and fraction. This helped to discuss the primary source of contaminant transfers (i.e., water and/or sediment), contaminant bioavailability, and the potentially harmful effects on biota.

For more information, see the Publication section of this website.

14th meeting Ecology and Behaviour (E&B) 2019

Details

The Ecology and Behaviour conferences are organized by and for young researchers. These events are an ideal opportunity for master, Ph.D.students, and postdocs to share their works with other researchers in ecology, evolutionary biology, and behavioral ecology.

As Co-organizer, chairman (Ecotoxicology & Ecophysiology session), and financial manager (Fundraising: 20k) of the 14th meeting Ecology and Behaviour (E&B) 2019, I was really proud of this great achievement which gathered more than 160 attendees and 14 keynote speakers around 56 platform presentations and 38 posters.
